Research

Current research.

Multimodal LLM-Based Interaction System for Home Robots with Real-Time Adaptation

PolyU URIS Sole student PI Sep 2025 – present

URIS is PolyU’s university-wide undergraduate research scheme. The project fuses voice, gesture, and visual context into one grounded dialogue loop for a robot operating in a home environment — supervised by Prof. Wei Lou.

For the perception layer I fine-tuned Qwen2.5-VL-7B with DoRA on RefCOCO grounding — 6 × Ascend 910B2, 8.1 hours — to give the system a reliable referring-expression backbone.

acc@0.5 78.8% → 91.2%  ·  mIoU 80.5  ·  1.62 s/query

SurveyLens · A Discipline-Aware Benchmark for Automatic Survey Generation

Co-author Under review · ACL ARR Sep 2025 – present

I built SurveyLens-1k — 1,000 human-written surveys, 100 per discipline across 10 fields — curated out of Semantic Scholar through keyword retrieval, LLM-based discipline tagging, and citation-rank pruning.

I then designed the three-stage cleaning pipeline that the dataset runs on (MinerU PDF parsing → rule filtering → LLM-assisted citation normalization), which lifted valid-sample retention from 62% to 94%.

PersonalPlan · Planning Multi-Agent Systems for Personalized Programming Learning

Co-author Under review · ACL ARR Mar 2026 – present

I built the plan-to-CrewAI runtime mapper and the four-axis execution-verification evaluator that gates plans into the MAP-PPL training set (3,043 query–profile–plan instances). The harness scores instruction fidelity, workflow completeness, interaction quality, and content correctness — running 100-plan batches inside a multi-language Docker sandbox.

I then validated GRPO Phase-1 reward training on Qwen3-8B + LoRA (RTX 5090, 156 steps): tier-1 mean reward moved 0.41 → 0.90. Along the way I traced the Qwen3 /no_think completion-truncation failure mode and wrote up a repro.

PolyInterview · AI Mock Interview Platform

A university initiative led by Prof. Jiannong Cao (PolyU VP for Research & Innovation), now live and in use by PolyU students. Four services — Flask API, Express auth, Vue frontend, and a LiveTalking digital-human avatar — running against self-hosted Qwen3 on local GPU, with STAR-style adaptive follow-up questioning.

On the build side I wrote JWT auth, the user and database modules, the resume-refinement step, and the VLM-based video evaluation; designed the three-tier feedback rubric (Features → Aspects → Proficiency); and wired Azure Neural TTS streaming into Wav2Lip lip-sync.

Qwen3 (self-hosted), Azure Neural TTS, Wav2Lip, WebRTC, Vue.js, Flask, Docker
